Pasco County has transformed dramatically over the past decade. What was once a collection of coastal retirement communities and rural inland towns is now one of the fastest-growing counties in the Tampa Bay area — up 17.3% in population since 2020. The growth has been concentrated in the southern corridor: Wesley Chapel, Land O' Lakes, and Zephyrhills have absorbed enormous waves of new residents priced out of Hillsborough and Pinellas counties to the south.

That growth, however, has created a two-speed market. In the high-growth southern tier, new construction dominates and entry-level homes move reasonably well. In the older coastal neighborhoods — Holiday, Port Richey, New Port Richey — the picture is different. These communities carry higher concentrations of aging housing stock, deferred maintenance, and long-term owners who are now in their 70s and 80s, facing decisions about what to do with properties that have appreciated significantly but require substantial investment to bring to market.

Pasco County's snowbird and retiree segment generates a consistent stream of absentee-owned properties — second homes purchased 15–20 years ago that heirs or estate executors need to move quickly without the complexity of a full listing. For sellers in this category, a direct sale is often faster, simpler, and produces a net result comparable to or better than a traditional listing after accounting for commissions, repairs, and carrying costs.

Common Questions

Pasco County — Frequently Asked Questions

Do you buy in the older coastal areas — New Port Richey, Holiday, Port Richey?

Yes. These neighborhoods represent a significant part of our activity in Pasco County. The housing stock is older, buyers using financing sometimes have trouble getting loans approved, and sellers often don't want to take on the repairs needed to list successfully. We buy these properties as-is.

The property I inherited has tenants in it. Can you still buy it?

Yes, we handle occupied properties. Whether the tenants are cooperative or not, we can structure the purchase accordingly — sometimes we close with the tenants in place and handle the transition ourselves. Reach out and describe the situation; we'll tell you straight whether it works for us and what the process would look like.

How does a remote closing work if I live out of state?

The title company prepares all documents. You can sign at a local notary, through a mobile notary who comes to you, or via remote online notarization (RON) which is legal in Florida. We coordinate with the title company to make the process as simple as possible — most out-of-state sellers find it straightforward.

Are you buying in Wesley Chapel and the growth corridor?

We're more active in older Pasco neighborhoods where the traditional market moves slowly. That said, we evaluate every property — if you have something in Wesley Chapel, Land O' Lakes, or Zephyrhills that fits our criteria, we'll make an offer. Reach out with the address and situation.
