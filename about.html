Clarity

What "Direct Buyer" Actually Means

A real estate agent lists your property on the MLS and tries to find a buyer who will pay the highest price. For that service, they take 5–6% of your sale price at closing — paid by you. The process involves showings, offers that fall through, inspection negotiations, and a timeline measured in months rather than weeks.

A direct buyer — which is what we are — doesn't list your property. We purchase it ourselves, with our own funds, directly from you. There's no MLS, no open houses, no buyer's financing that might fall through at the last minute. We do our research, make you an offer, and if you accept, we close. That's the whole transaction.

The trade-off is straightforward: our offer will be below what a perfectly prepped property might fetch at peak retail, because we're taking on the cost and risk of the repairs and the resale. What you're buying with that difference is speed, certainty, and the ability to walk away from the property without spending another dollar on it.

That trade-off is the right one for a lot of sellers. Not all of them — and we'll tell you honestly if we think listing traditionally would serve you better. But for sellers who need to move quickly, who can't or don't want to invest in repairs, or who need certainty over top dollar, we're built for exactly that situation.

How We Work

Three Things You Can Count On

A Real Offer, Not a Starting Point

The number we present is what we'll close on. We don't offer high to get you under contract and then renegotiate down after the inspection. If something we discover changes the math, we'll tell you — but we don't play games with the number.

Straight Talk About Whether We Can Help

Not every property works for us. If yours doesn't — whether the numbers don't pencil out or the situation isn't something we can solve — we'll tell you that directly and won't waste your time. Honest "no" is more useful than a runaround.

No Pressure, Ever

You can take our offer and think about it. There's no deadline to decide, no follow-up calls pressuring you to sign. If you want to talk through the numbers or compare your options, we'll have that conversation. The decision is yours.
